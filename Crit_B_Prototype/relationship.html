Affordable and Clean Energy in Sustainable Cities?


Cities account for approximately 3-5% of the Earth's surface, but in fact contribute to 60% of energy consumption and approximately 70% of CO2 emissions. A sustainable city must be able to sustain itself whilst relying on the environment around it. Hence it must power itself with renewable energy sources in order to avoid overconsumption of resources. It must also create a small ecological footprint and a low amount of pollution.

Hence, as seen above, in order to meet the requirements of SDG 11, it is necessary to implement some of the goals as outlined in SDG 7 in order to implement affordable and clean energy within the city

In terms of the automotive industry in particular, sustainable transportation emphasizes a variety of energy efficient transportation systems in order to not only reduce greenhouse gas emissions, but also, to meet demand for fuel and energy. Because the price of energy is extremely volatile, a system like this would attempt to ensure that the price of gas will become more stable, as there are a variety of fuel sources, and the method of generation is efficent and consistent.

The utilization of alternative energy cars and installation of refuelling stations has attracted increasing importance. Tesla is one of the pioneers in the development of electric vehicles which is said to reduce the carbon footprint of cars. However, more and more companies (such as Arrival/Nio) are developing electric vehicles and public transportation systems to improve sustainable transportation.





Solutions Presented to Companies




  • Local Energy: Community energy, district heating schemes, combined heat and power, and heat pumps are all suggested
  • Solar Energy: Utilization of dye-sensitive photovoltaic coatings on buildings to generate electricity has been experimented by Tata Steel. Surfaces do not need to point at the sun to be efficient, and panels do not take up land space.
  • Anaerobic Digestion: Power can come from anaerobic digestion of organic and green waste (Produces natural gas and can be injected into mains) used in combined heat and power plants or to power transport.
