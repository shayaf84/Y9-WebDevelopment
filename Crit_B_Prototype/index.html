Affordable and Clean Energy

SDG7 SDG11 Comparison


"Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all"


Between 2000 and 2018, the number of people posessing electicity increased from 78% to 90%, while the population without electricity dropped to 789 million.

However, the population continues to grow, and with it, the demand for cheap energy. Greater attention is required in order to ameliorate access to clean and safe cooking fuels and technology for 3 billion people, to widen the utilization of renewable energy, and to increase electrification in sub-Saharan Africa. Furthermore, an economy dependent on fossil fuels is creating dire effects on the climate through greenhouse gas emissions

Investing in solar, wind, thermal, and other renewable energy sources, ensuring energy for all, as well as improving energy productivity is crucial if our goal is to achieve SDG 7 by 2030.

Expanding infrastructure and uprgrading technoloby to provide clean, and more efficient energy (which all countries are able to access) will augment growth and assist the environment

The COVID-19 pandemic outlined the need for reliable and affordable electricity in healthcare centres. Furthermore, a survery conducted in certain developing countries dictated that that 1/4 of health facilities were not equipped with electricity, and another quarter had unscheduled power outages, which weakened their ability to deliver essential health services, weakening the health system's response to the crisis.





Solutions Presented to Companies




  • Collaborate across the industry and with governments in order to improve intermodal and trans-modal transfer systems with the goal of augmenting energy efficiency.
  • Upgrade fleets to enable the utilization of alternative, less-carbon intensive fuel, and propel the use of renewable sources of energy
  • Work with manufacturers in order to ameliorate the design and energy performance of vehicles, airplanes, and ships
  • Collaborate with industry bodies, academia, the energy sector, and the government to make coordinated investments in the R&D of next generation biofuels with the goal of decreasing the time in which it becomes commercially viable.

Case Study 1: Jaguar Land Rover


  • Opened a new engine manufacturing location in the UK with a solar-array placed on the roof.
  • Has 22,622 panels which can supply 30% of the site's energy needs (can plan 1600 homes)
  • 2400 tonnes of CO2 per year was reduced.
  • The company invested $50 USD in improving energy performance through an "integrated approach of efficiency, process change and renewable energy".

Case Study 2: Kandi EV CarShare


  • Car sharing program of Kandi Technology Group in Hangzhou, China
  • Operates only Kandi all-Electric Cars which is available in vending-machine like autonomous garages
  • $3.25 /hour rental price and $130-160 / month leasing including insurance, maintenance, electric batteries
  • Expanded to Shanghai, Chengdu, Wuhan, etc.
