Sustainable Cities and Communities

SDG7 SDG11 Comparison


"Make cities inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable".


It is predicted that the world will undergo increased urbanization. Since 2007, more than 50% of the global population has been living in cities, and that number is predicted to rise to 60% by 2030.

Cities, metropolitan, and urban areas are central to economic growth, as they contribute to approximately 60% of the world's GDP. However, they also account for 60% of global CO2 emissions, and utilize over 70% of resources.

Urban Sprawl and Rapid Urbanization augment the number of people who live in slums, unsatisfactory and overburdened infrastructure and services (waste collection, water sanitation, etc.), which thus can worsen air pollution.

The COVID-19 pandemic will be devastating in low-income and densely populated communities, especially considering 1 billion people live in slums worldwide, in which ovecrowding of population can increase the difficulty of following public health measures such as physical distancing.

This page will attempt to give an overview to automotive companies about the goal in general, and how they can take action. Different companies have taken different action. For example, carsharing is a solution that was taken by corporations, as well as the construction of hybrid/electric cars with the goal of reducing emissions and easing urban traffic. For example, Ferrovial's Zity carsharing service has prevented the emission of approx. 1500 tonnes of CO2 in the atmosphere.





Solutions Presented to Companies





  • Cooperate with governments and other stakeholders to ameliorate road, rail, and marine safety.
  • Partner and take action with industry members and city planners to support sustainable transport solutions to enhance mobility of vulnerable people (low income families, people with reduced mobility, etc.)
  • Cooperate with national and regional planner to help improve transportation links between urban, suburban, and rural areas, which can assist the creation of new socio-economic opportunities (eg: access to employment/markets)
  • Develop technology and work with other transportation providers in order to allow for amalgamated origin to destination transport.
  • Share anonymized data with governments (eg: reveals any trends about the automotive industry) to assist in managing urban growth, decrease traffic congestion, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Case Study 1: FedEx


  • Embarked on a project with EMBARQ (a think tank) with the objective of identifying sustainable public transport solutions in cities in India, Mexico, and Brazil
  • Reached out to over 1600 transport officials and drivers, and helped to reduce 20,000 tons of CO2 emissions.
  • Helped urban transport operators to provide enhanced an efficient survices

Case Study 2: General Motors


  • Launched Maven: A new brand and business model for car sharing.
  • Offers car sharing to anyone with a Maven smartphone app in a city where Maven is available.
  • Pricing is simple and transparent, which includes insurance and fuel.
